Playtested the new initiative system with the usual group last week. The old
d20-roll-and-sort approach was fine on paper but kept producing the same
"everyone waits for the slow player to figure out their turn" drag at the
table, so I gutted it.

New system: at the start of combat, everyone draws from a shared pool of
initiative cards instead of rolling. Cards get spent as you act, so tension
builds as the pool thins out toward the end of the round.

Early verdict: rounds move faster and nobody's staring at their phone waiting
for turn six. Still need to figure out how surprise rounds interact with the
draw pool.
